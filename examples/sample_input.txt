The quiet village sat at the edge of the world, where the mountains met the sea and the road simply ended. No one had ever mapped what lay beyond the last lighthouse, and for generations, no one had tried.

Then, one autumn morning, a letter arrived. It bore no postmark and no return address — only a single line, written in silver ink: "Come when the tide is lowest. Bring what you cannot leave behind."

Mira read it three times. She folded it carefully, tucked it into the pocket of her grandmother's coat, and walked to the shore. The gulls were strangely quiet. Somewhere out past the breakers, something was waiting.
